Results:

AUC from approach 1 (using TF/IDF weights) is around 0.698 which is same as the one using DTM. In this case, adding TF/IDF weights did not increase model performance. Could be because the presence and frequency of terms tells us more about the class of document (i.e its sentiment) than the individual document.

AUC from approach 2 (using Ridge regularization) is around 0.65 which is lower than original model (which uses Lasso regression). This is because the Lasso tends to do well if there are a small number of significant parameters and the others are close to zero (can be confirmed from histograms).

AUC from approach 3 (using higher threshold) is around 0.76 which is higher than original model. This is due to class imbalance in our training dataset. Training dataset is dominated with positive ratings and hence increasing threshold improves our model by decreasing false positives.

AUC from approach 4 (increasing nfolds in cross validation) is 0.7 around which is slightly higher than original model. Increasing number of folds is computationally expensive but it also ensures that our model does not overfit which is a possibility in this case due to class imbalance.

AUC from approach 5 (using Ridge regularization) is around 0.83 which is higher than original model. As undersampling approach aids the task of binary classification in the presence of rare classes. It produces a synthetic, possibly balanced, sample of data simulated according to a smoothed-bootstrap approach.Thus provides better estimates.
